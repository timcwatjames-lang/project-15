🗣️ Introducing Yourself

Basic Pattern: "My name is [Name]. I am from [Country]. I am a [Profession/Student]."

Examples:

  • My name is John. I am from Nigeria. I am a student.
  • My name is Sarah. I am from America. I am a teacher.
  • My name is Ahmed. I am from Egypt. I am a doctor.

📖 Rule 1: Subject-Verb Agreement

In English, the subject and verb must match. Use is with singular subjects and are with plural subjects.

  • He is a student. (singular)
  • They are students. (plural)
  • She is happy.
  • We are friends.

📖 Rule 2: Using Articles (a, an, the)

  • a - Used before consonant sounds: a book, a car, a teacher
  • an - Used before vowel sounds: an apple, an egg, an idea
  • the - Used for specific things: the sun, the book on the table

📖 Rule 3: This/That - These/Those

  • This - For singular items near you: This is my book.
  • That - For singular items far from you: That is her house.
  • These - For plural items near you: These are my friends.
  • Those - For plural items far from you: Those are their cars.

⏰ English Tenses Overview

Understanding tenses is crucial for expressing time in English. Here are the 12 tenses:

📖 Present Tenses

  • Simple Present: I play football every Sunday. (Habits, facts)
  • Present Continuous: I am playing football now. (Happening now)
  • Present Perfect: I have played football before. (Past action, present result)
  • Present Perfect Continuous: I have been playing for 2 hours. (Continuous from past to now)

📖 Past Tenses

  • Simple Past: I played football yesterday. (Completed actions)
  • Past Continuous: I was playing when it rained. (Action in progress in past)
  • Past Perfect: I had finished before he came. (Action before another past action)
  • Past Perfect Continuous: I had been playing for an hour. (Continuous past action)

📖 Future Tenses

  • Simple Future: I will play football tomorrow. (Predictions, decisions)
  • Future Continuous: I will be playing at 5 PM. (Action at specific future time)
  • Future Perfect: I will have finished by tomorrow. (Action completed by future time)
  • Future Perfect Continuous: I will have been playing for 3 hours. (Continuous future action)

📖 Conditionals

  • Zero Conditional: If + present simple, present simple (Facts)
    If you heat water to 100°C, it boils.
  • First Conditional: If + present simple, will + verb (Real possibilities)
    If it rains, I will stay home.
  • Second Conditional: If + past simple, would + verb (Imaginary)
    If I won the lottery, I would travel the world.
  • Third Conditional: If + past perfect, would have + past participle (Past imaginary)
    If I had studied harder, I would have passed.

📖 Passive Voice

Formula: Subject + be + past participle

  • Active: The chef prepares the food.
  • Passive: The food is prepared by the chef.
  • Active: They built this house in 2020.
  • Passive: This house was built in 2020.
  • Active: Someone will complete the project.
  • Passive: The project will be completed.

📖 Modal Verbs for Probability

  • Must/Could: He must be at home. (Strong probability)
  • Might/May: She might come later. (Possibility)
  • Can't: He can't be serious. (Impossibility)
  • Should: He should be there by now. (Expectation)

📖 Reported Speech (Indirect Speech)

Key Rules: When reporting speech, move tense back (backshift)

  • Direct: "I am happy." → Reported: He said he was happy.
  • Direct: "I will come tomorrow." → Reported: He said he would come the next day.
  • Direct: "I have finished." → Reported: She said she had finished.
  • Direct: "I saw him yesterday." → Reported: They said they saw him the day before.
  • Direct: "I am studying English." → Reported: He said he was studying English.

📖 Inversions (Advanced Sentence Structure)

Use inversions to make your English more sophisticated:

  • Never have I seen such a beautiful sunset.
  • Rarely does he complain about anything.
  • Seldom do we go to the cinema.
  • Only after the meeting did I understand the situation.
  • Not only is she talented, but she is also kind.
  • Had I known, I would have helped you. (conditional inversion)

📖 Cleft Sentences

Emphasize information using cleft sentences:

  • It was John who broke the window. (emphasizing subject)
  • What I need is a good night's sleep. (emphasizing subject)
  • The reason I called was to invite you. (giving reason)
  • All I want is peace and quiet. (emphasizing desire)
